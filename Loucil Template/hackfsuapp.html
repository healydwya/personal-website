HackFSU Judging App



I had the wonderful opportunity of working as a web developer for HackFSU 2016, during which I gained many fun late night memories of working on the site at Domi Station with the TechNole & HackFSU crew, as well as knowledge of working on the front-end of a Node.js framework for our judging web app. I collaborated with a team of developers to build HackFSU 2016's judging app, which I contributed to using Jade, CSS, and Javascript.

Unfortunately, my GitHub account was not configured properly with my local Git repo at the time, so my contributions do not show online. However, if you are interested in learning how we built the app, click here to see our now publicly available source code!

This was the third HackFSU that I have been a part of (2nd time as a coordinator) and I am thankful to have contributed to a product that will be used at future hackathons!
