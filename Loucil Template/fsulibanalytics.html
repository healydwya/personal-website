FSU Libraries Physical Usage
Analytics


This data visualization project is something I built for work as a Data Analyst for the Assessment Office of FSU Libraries. My passion and frustrations with big data led me to the back-then emerging, but now very popular software, called Tableau, which is now being widely used to visualize different kinds of university data at work. This visualization represents hundreds and thousands of rows of data collected from the library turnstiles, which I collect monthly from FSUPD's C-Cure Facility Software.

What I really aimed to accomplish from creating this visualization is to show the overall importance of the libraries to our students at FSU, especially during finals week. What you don't see on this dashboard is a spreadsheet with thousands of rows of unformatted data, but what you do see is a dashboard that represents the changes that go on throughout the semester. It gives you a glimpse of how students use the library throughout the academic year. That is what is so great about Tableau. Further analyses and improvement is being done on this, and I am currently building a data dashboard for internal use, which presents an in-depth stats overview of how students truly use and spend their time at our libraries.
